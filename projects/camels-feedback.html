Quantifying Baryonic Feedback on the Warm‚ÄìHot Circumgalactic Medium in CAMELS Simulations

Publication: ApJ (2025)

In this study, we use data from the CAMELS simulation suite to explore how different subgrid implementations of stellar and AGN feedback affect the distribution of baryons in and around halos. Our focus is on the warm‚Äìhot circumgalactic medium (CGM), a phase that encodes key signatures of galactic outflows.

Figure 1. Halo baryon content across different CAMELS models, colored by feedback prescriptions. The vertical dashed line marks the closure radius.

We introduce the concept of a closure radius ‚Äî the radius at which the baryonic mass enclosed matches the halo‚Äôs expected cosmic baryon fraction. This metric captures how far feedback drives baryons into the CGM or beyond.

Interestingly, we find that SIMBA's feedback model, while injecting less total energy than IllustrisTNG, more effectively displaces gas beyond the halo boundary. This suggests SIMBA's coupling to the surrounding medium is more efficient.

Our results highlight the importance of feedback implementation details, and suggest new ways to observationally constrain them via CGM structure and scaling relations.
